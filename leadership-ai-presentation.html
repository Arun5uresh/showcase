A note for leadership

The shift isn't capability.
It's who can act on it.

What changed with AI is not just what these systems can do — it's who can now put them to work. This note is about that shift, and what it asks of us.

03 The evolution

Six waves, each compressing the distance between a question and an answer.

Wave 01 Descriptive what happened
Wave 02 Diagnostic why it happened
Wave 03 Predictive what will happen
Wave 04 ML at scale what to decide
Wave 05 Generative what to create
Wave 06 Agentic what to do

The last two waves are not just faster analytics. They're a different kind of tool — one that interacts, generates, and increasingly acts.

04 The inflection

These systems don't just analyze.

interact. generate. act.

This is categorically different from anything that came before. Leaders now have access to tools that don't just inform a decision — they can carry parts of it out.

05 Democratization

Three things converged to put these tools in reach of non-specialists.

01

Natural language

Interfaces replaced code. A well-formed question is now often enough to get a working answer.

02

Composable tooling

Capabilities snap together like blocks. Models, retrieval, memory, and actions now compose, not collide.

03

Platforms

Production-grade AI is one prompt away. The last mile between idea and deployment is shorter than it's ever been.

In practice Databricks Microsoft Copilot ChatGPT & the tools your team already uses
06 The new expectation

Leaders are not expected to become engineers. They're expected to operate with fluency.

Not expected

To build from scratch

No one is asking leaders to write models, tune hyperparameters, or ship production code themselves. Depth of technical skill is not the bar.

Expected

To operate with fluency

Breadth of applied understanding. Knowing what these tools can do, where they fit, and how to put them to work on real problems.

07 Five practical capabilities

What fluent operation looks like, concretely.

01

Frame

Pose problems in a way AI systems can actually act on.

02

Assemble

Orchestrate workflows from existing tools — not code, compose.

03

Extract

Pull insights directly from data, without routing through intermediaries.

04

Identify

Spot where decisions can be automated or augmented, and where they shouldn't.

05

Scale

Spread capability across teams — as defaults, not as isolated pilots.
